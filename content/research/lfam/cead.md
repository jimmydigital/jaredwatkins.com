## Company Overview

CEAD B.V. (Delft, Netherlands) designs and manufactures robotic LFAM systems for thermoplastic composite production. The company's Flexbot platform — a 6-axis industrial robot equipped with a large-format pellet extruder — is notable for continuous fiber reinforcement capability: while most LFAM systems use chopped carbon fiber in thermoplastic pellets, CEAD has developed technology to integrate continuous fiber tapes during deposition, significantly improving mechanical properties for structural applications.

- **HQ:** Delft, Netherlands
- **Founded:** 2016
- **Key system:** Flexbot / AM Flexbot
- **Differentiator:** Continuous fiber reinforcement in robotic LFAM; Maritime Application Center
- **Model:** Direct machine sales plus in-house production service

## Flexbot Platform

The Flexbot mounts a pellet extrusion head on a 6-axis robot (typically KUKA), with optional 7th-axis linear rails for larger parts. Configuration options include:

- **Print head:** Single or dual pellet extruder; high-temperature capable
- **Continuous fiber:** Optional integrated continuous fiber placement during deposition — fibers laid in the part as it prints, not just embedded in pellet feedstock
- **Cell size:** Scalable from benchtop to production cell; multiple robots can be deployed in a microfactory configuration
- **Materials:** CF-PP, CF-PA, CF-PEI, CF-PEEK, and continuous CF/GF tape-reinforced variants
- **Applications:** Marine hull tooling, automotive structural parts, industrial jigs, aerospace components

## Maritime Application Center

CEAD launched the Maritime Application Center specifically to develop LFAM technology for boatbuilding and marine structural production. The center focuses on:

- Full-scale boat hull production by LFAM — printing hull molds or direct structural hulls
- Material qualification for marine environments (moisture resistance, salt, UV)
- Development of LFAM process parameters for thermoplastics suited to marine use (CF-PP and CF-PA for corrosion resistance, CF-PEEK for high-performance applications)
- Industry partnerships with boatbuilders and marine composite manufacturers

The Maritime Application Center positions CEAD at the intersection of two converging trends: the marine industry's slow adoption of thermoplastic composites (vs. thermoset GFRP), and LFAM's need for end-use applications beyond aerospace tooling.

## Automotive Partnership

CEAD has collaborated with DAF Trucks on 3D-printed composite parts — a commercial vehicle application that demonstrates the Flexbot's ability to produce large structural thermoplastic parts outside of tooling contexts. This signals CEAD's ambition to move from tooling supplier to structural parts producer.

## Competitive Position

In robotic LFAM, CEAD competes primarily with Caracol AM (Italy) and Rapid Fusion (UK). CEAD differentiates on:
- Continuous fiber capability (Caracol and Rapid Fusion use chopped-fiber pellets only)
- Maritime Application Center as a dedicated development and customer facility
- European machine tool pedigree with strong composites industry network
