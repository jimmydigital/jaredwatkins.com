## Company Overview

Thermwood Corporation (Dale, Indiana) is a US CNC machine tool manufacturer that developed the LSAM (Large Scale Additive Manufacturing) system — the dominant gantry-based LFAM platform for thermoplastic composite tooling in the aerospace and defense market. Thermwood is privately held and has been making CNC routers since 1969; LSAM was introduced around 2016 as a natural extension of their large-format machining expertise.

The LSAM's competitive position rests on a combined print-then-trim workflow: the same gantry machine that 3D prints large composite parts can flip to CNC trimming mode, producing finished near-net-shape parts without moving to a separate machining center. This near-net workflow is critical for autoclave cure tooling, where dimensional accuracy determines part quality.

## LSAM System Family

### LSAM (Full Combined Systems)
Dual-gantry machines that both print (additive) and trim (subtractive) on the same platform. Available in table sizes ranging from 10×5 ft to 40+ ft lengths. Capable of printing parts up to ~10 ft tall.

- **Print head:** High-temperature pellet extruder; up to 450°C processing temperature
- **Control:** Ultra 6 CNC control (same platform across all LSAM variants)
- **Materials:** CF-ABS, CF-Nylon, CF-PEI (ULTEM), CF-PEEK, CF-PEKK, and virtually any composite thermoplastic pellet
- **Deposition rate:** ~100–150 kg/hr depending on material and head configuration
- **Applications:** Autoclave cure tooling, lay-up molds, patterns, structural parts

### LSAM AP510 (Additive Printer Only)
Lower-cost print-only variant introduced to broaden market access. Fixed gantry, moving table design.

- **Table sizes:** 5×5 ft and 5×10 ft
- **Max part height:** ~4 ft
- **Print head:** Same as full LSAM; same Ultra 6 control
- **Target market:** R&D departments, job shops, lower-volume tooling production
- **Notable demo:** JEC World 2026 live print of matched drone mold pairs from CF-polycarbonate

## Materials Ecosystem

Thermwood collaborates with material suppliers to qualify new pellet feedstocks for LSAM. JEC World 2026 demo used CF-polycarbonate from Airtech, SABIC, and Techmer PM simultaneously — demonstrating multi-supplier material qualification. The LSAM system can process virtually any thermoplastic composite pellet at temperatures up to 450°C.

## Competitive Position

Thermwood's main competition in gantry-based LFAM is Ingersoll Machine Tools (MasterPrint). Thermwood differentiates on:
- Combined print+trim in one machine (Ingersoll requires separate machining)
- Extensive aerospace customer validation (Boeing, AFRL programs)
- US-manufactured system (important for defense procurement)
- Lower entry price via LSAM AP510
