## Summary

Harmonic Drive Systems Inc. (Tokyo Stock Exchange: 6324), headquartered in Tokyo, is the world's leading manufacturer of strain wave gearing — the high-precision, zero-backlash gear mechanism that is the dominant joint transmission technology in industrial robot arms, collaborative robots, surgical robots, and increasingly humanoid robots. Founded in 1970, the company holds a wide-moat competitive position (Morningstar designation) as the inventor and primary commercial licensor of the harmonic drive gearing principle. FY2024/3 (ended March 2024) revenue was ¥28.6 billion (~$190M USD). The FY2025/3 forecast was ¥34 billion. 1H FY2026/3 (through September 2025) revenue was ¥27.8 billion, up 4.8% year-over-year — ahead of expectations. The humanoid robot build-out represents a structural demand driver: Tesla's Optimus robot uses 14 harmonic drive joints per unit, and large-scale humanoid production could multiply total market size.

## What It Is / How It Works

The harmonic drive (strain wave gearing) mechanism consists of three components: a wave generator (elliptical bearing), a flexspline (thin flexible cup with external teeth), and a circular spline (rigid ring with internal teeth). The wave generator deforms the flexspline so its teeth engage with the circular spline at two diametrically opposed points; as the wave generator rotates, the engagement points travel around the gear, producing a very high gear reduction ratio (typically 30:1 to 320:1) in an extraordinarily compact package. The key properties that make harmonic drives critical for robotics are: zero backlash (critical for precision positioning repeatability), high torque density (high torque output per unit mass/volume), and smooth transmission (no cogging). These properties cannot be matched by conventional spur, helical, or even planetary gearing at comparable size and weight.

Every articulated industrial robot arm — from Fanuc to ABB to KUKA — uses harmonic drives or similar strain wave gearboxes in its wrist and elbow joints, where precision and compact packaging are most critical. Harmonic Drive Systems is the dominant supplier to this market globally. Competing strain wave gear manufacturers exist (particularly Chinese makers such as Leaderdrive and LSSG), but Harmonic Drive Systems retains its position in premium robots and sensitive applications (surgical, semiconductor, space) where its quality certification and track record are not easily displaced.

The humanoid robot demand thesis centers on joint count: a typical humanoid robot requires 25–50 actuated joints, many of which use harmonic drives. If humanoid robot production reaches meaningful volume — Tesla has projected Optimus production at potentially millions of units per year — the implied harmonic drive demand would exceed current total market size, requiring either massive capacity expansion from HDS, entry of competitive suppliers, or substitution with alternative gearing. The capacity expansion announced by HDS (doubling reduction gear capacity by 2026) signals the company's own assessment that demand will exceed prior peaks.

The semiconductor equipment market (photolithography, wafer handling arms) provides a second significant revenue stream — precision semiconductor equipment uses harmonic drives in wafer positioning and robot arm joints, creating cross-market diversification.

## Supply Chain Position

Harmonic Drive Systems is a **Component-Subsystem Supplier** at the precision gearbox layer — the enabling transmission component between motor and robot joint. It sits one level below robot OEMs (who buy HDS gearboxes to integrate with motors) and one level above raw material suppliers (steel alloys, precision grinding consumables). The company's production is vertically integrated for the precision gear machining steps that define quality.

**⚑ Shared supplier with Nabtesco:** HDS (harmonic/strain wave) and Nabtesco (cycloidal RV reducers) compete for the same robot joint application; industrial robot OEMs typically use HDS gearboxes in wrist/small joints and Nabtesco RV reducers in shoulder/base joints (higher torque, larger diameter). Both are Japanese, creating a geographic concentration risk for global robot supply chains.

**⚑ Chinese competition entering:** Leaderdrive, LSSG, and other Chinese harmonic drive makers have entered the low-to-mid quality range. Cost pressure on Chinese-assembled collaborative robots and humanoid robots may accelerate substitution of HDS gearboxes by domestic Chinese alternatives in that market segment, while HDS retains premium segment.

**⚑ Rare earth dependency note:** HDS gearboxes themselves are steel/alloy components without inherent rare earth dependency. However, the motors that drive HDS gearboxes (from maxon, Kollmorgen, Moog, or commodity BLDC suppliers) use NdFeB magnets — the system-level rare earth exposure is in the motor layer, not in HDS gearboxes specifically.
