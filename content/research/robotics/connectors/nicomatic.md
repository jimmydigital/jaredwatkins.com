## Summary

Nicomatic is a French, family-owned designer and manufacturer of micro electrical connectors and cables for civil and military aerospace, defense, and security applications, with a particular focus on UAVs and robotics. Its DMM, CMM, and EMM rugged micro connector families, flat flexible cable (FFC) products, and newer Matr'YX high-speed connector range are used to carry power, signal, and video between PCBs and cable assemblies in weight- and vibration-constrained platforms.

## Key Facts

- Founded: over 40 years of operating history as of 2024/2025 (implies founding in the early-to-mid 1980s); headquartered in Bons-en-Chablais, France
- Type: Component/subsystem supplier — connector and interconnect cable manufacturer

- Value chain position: Component/subsystem supplier (connectors and cabling for UAV/defense/aerospace OEMs and integrators)

## What It Is / How It Works

Nicomatic's product range spans several tiers of interconnect solution depending on the vibration/altitude/weight trade-off a customer needs. Flexible Flat Cables (FFC) are the lightest, lowest-cost option, compatible with ZIF (zero insertion force) connectors, suited to low-vibration environments. CrimpFlex adds a patented six-point crimping technology for vibration resistance while remaining lightweight, available in 2.54 mm and 1.27 mm pitch supporting up to 3 amps per contact. Microflex is a hybrid system combining flat cable with round-cable-style CMM screw connectors (meeting MIL-DTL-55302F) for high-altitude, high-vibration aerospace and space applications.

For the most demanding environments, Nicomatic's DMM, CMM, and EMM connector families are rugged, MIL-standard-exceeding rectangular micro connectors: DMM connectors are claimed to be up to 40% lighter than standard Micro-D while offering "millions of standard configurations," mixed contact types in a single connector body, and superior EMI/RFI and mechanical protection. The EMM family specifically targets high-speed data transmission protocols (Ethernet, USB 3, SATA III) with 4–60 signal contacts, 20% lighter than standard Micro-Ds. In 2025, Nicomatic introduced Matr'YX, a newer ultra-compact, high-density connector aimed at extreme environments and high-performance embedded systems, supporting data rates up to 25 Gbps (PAM-4).

All of Nicomatic's core connector manufacturing is performed in-house, which the company markets as enabling fast (two-week) turnaround on many standard micro rugged connector configurations — a contrast to competitors who may outsource stamping, plating, or molding steps.
