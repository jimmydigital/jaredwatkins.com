## Summary

Samtec is a privately held, family-owned connector manufacturer headquartered in New Albany, Indiana, best known in the robotics/drone context for its micro rugged, high-speed board-to-board connector systems (Edge Rate ERF6/ERM6), which carry sensor and compute data links at up to 112 Gbps PAM4 between mezzanine boards in space-constrained platforms.

## Key Facts

- Founded: 1976, by Sam Shine (with his wife Betty as the company's only other early employee)
- Type: Component/subsystem supplier — connector and cable assembly manufacturer
- Status: Active, privately and family-held; current CEO is Sam Shine's son, John Shine
- Key metric(s): ERF6/ERM6 Edge Rate connectors rated for 56 Gbps PAM4 mezzanine applications; 5 mm mated height, 2.5 mm body width, 11–42.8 mm body length, 10/20/30/40/50/60 positions per row; flexible power options from 3 to 60 Amps; IP67/IP68 sealed variants available
- Value chain position: Component/subsystem supplier (high-speed and rugged board-to-board/power connectors for robotics, drone, and industrial/embedded-vision OEMs)

## What It Is / How It Works

Samtec's core relevance to robotics and drones is its micro rugged interconnect portfolio: board-to-board and mezzanine connector systems built for high signal-integrity performance in small, high-vibration packages. The ERF6 and ERM6 Edge Rate connectors, introduced in March 2024, are two-row, high-density mezzanine connectors half the width of prior designs, with a low-profile 5 mm mated height, 2.5 mm body width, and body lengths from 11 to 42.8 mm (0.635 mm centerline; 10/20/30/40/50/60 positions per row) — designed for 56 Gbps PAM4 rugged mezzanine applications including industrial embedded vision, instrumentation and monitoring, drones, and robotics. Optional weld tabs resist pull and shear force, and molded polarizing features tolerate angular and linear misalignment during mating. Samtec markets its "Tiger Eye" and "Edge Rate" contact systems specifically for high mating-cycle count and shock/vibration resistance, distinguishing them from consumer-grade board-to-board connectors that are not rated for rugged environments.

Beyond pure high-speed signal connectors, Samtec's "rugged power" line supplies board-to-board power connectors from 3 to 60 Amps with IP67/68 sealing, letting integrators combine power and data connectivity across the same micro rugged connector family rather than sourcing them from separate vendors. This combination — rugged high-speed data plus scalable power in a common mechanical system — mirrors the hybrid power+data trend seen in Fischer Connectors' MiniMax series ([Fischer Connectors]({{< relref "fischer-connectors.md" >}})), though Samtec's products are primarily board-to-board rather than cable-to-cable/panel circular connectors.

Samtec remains privately and family-held nearly 50 years after founding, a similar ownership pattern to Fischer Connectors and Nicomatic among the specialist manufacturers tracked in this subsection, and a contrast to the conglomerate-owned tier (Positronic/Amphenol, Cinch/Bel Fuse).
