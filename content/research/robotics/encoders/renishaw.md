## Summary

Renishaw plc is a British precision engineering company, headquartered near Wotton-under-Edge, England, founded in 1973 by Sir David McMurtry and John Deer around a patented touch-trigger probe for coordinate-measuring machines. Its product portfolio has since expanded into optical and magnetic position encoders (including for robotics), CNC machine tool calibration, additive manufacturing machines, dental CAD/CAM, and medical devices for neurosurgery. Renishaw also holds a 50% ownership stake in Slovenia-based magnetic encoder specialist [RLS]({{< relref "rls.md" >}}).

## What It Is / How It Works

Renishaw's encoder product line for robotics spans optical (ATOM™ miniature incremental encoders) and magnetic (AksIM™ off-axis absolute encoders, Orbis™ through-hole encoders, RM08 miniature encoders, LinACE™ cylindrical linear encoders) technologies, marketed across use cases including wafer-handling robots in semiconductor fabrication, microelectronics assembly, pick-and-place robotics, high-speed packaging, and surgical robotics. Renishaw's own published case studies describe magnetic encoders (AksIM, Orbis) integrated into the knee, wrist, and elbow joints of PAL Robotics' humanoid balance-control robot, and miniature encoders used in Marsi Bionics exoskeletons, where minimizing weight and volume without compromising reliability was the central design constraint — a direct echo of the space/weight trade-offs found throughout drone component subsystems covered elsewhere in this research area.

Renishaw's origins are in an unrelated but foundational precision-metrology invention: founder Sir David McMurtry, while working to measure fuel pipes on a prototype jet engine, found existing coordinate-measuring machine touch probes too imprecise for delicate components and invented a spring-loaded kinematic touch-trigger probe, which he patented — the seed product for the company's later diversification into encoders, CNC calibration, additive manufacturing, and medical devices. Distinct from its own directly-branded encoder lines, Renishaw holds a 50% ownership stake in [RLS]({{< relref "rls.md" >}}) (Komenda, Slovenia), a magnetic encoder specialist, and sells RLS-designed encoders through its global sales and applications-engineering network — meaning "Renishaw" and "RLS" branded magnetic encoders can, for some product lines, trace back to shared design and manufacturing roots.
