## Summary

Xoar International is a propeller manufacturer headquartered in Arcadia, California, producing beechwood and carbon fiber propellers for RC aircraft, multicopters, and drones, and also selling matched Titan-brand brushless motors and Pulse-brand ESCs — positioning itself as a source for a complete propulsion system rather than propellers alone.

## Key Facts

- Type: Component/subsystem supplier — propeller, motor, and ESC manufacturer
- Status: Active, privately held
- Headquarters: Arcadia, California, USA (per third-party business directories; not independently confirmed against a primary Xoar corporate-address page in this session)
- Key metric(s): Carbon fiber precision-pair multi-rotor propellers claimed to increase flight time up to 20% compared to plastic propellers (per company marketing); product range spans beechwood (PJP-N series) and carbon fiber (PJP-T-L series, including folding and ultralight variants) multicopter propellers, plus direct-replacement propellers for DJI Phantom 3/4 and Inspire 1/2
- Value chain position: Component/subsystem supplier (propellers, motors, and ESCs for drone/RC OEMs, integrators, and end users), with some vertical integration across the propulsion stack (Titan motors, Pulse ESCs, propellers under one brand)

## What It Is / How It Works

Xoar's core multicopter propeller line is organized as "Precision Pair" sets differentiated by material and drivetrain: beechwood props (PJP-N, PJP-N-M with metallic coating) for standard electric or gas multicopter/fixed-wing use, and carbon fiber props (PJP-T-L and variants, including folding PJP-T-LF and ultralight PJP-T-LU versions) purpose-built for low-Kv electric motors, where the material's superior strength-to-weight ratio yields the company's claimed flight-time improvement. Beyond generic propeller sizes, Xoar also sells direct-replacement propellers engineered specifically for popular consumer/prosumer drone platforms (DJI Phantom 3/4, Inspire 1/2), targeting the aftermarket-parts segment of the drone market.

Distinct from single-component propeller specialists like APC Propellers, Xoar also markets its own Titan-brand brushless motors (Titan and Titan Air series, spanning heavy-lift to lightweight-pro configurations) and Pulse-brand ESCs, explicitly framing the combination of Titan motor, Pulse ESC, and Xoar propeller as "the ultimate propulsion system available in the market" — a smaller-scale version of the vertical integration strategy also used by Chinese suppliers Hobbywing and T-Motor (see [Electronic Speed Controllers]({{< relref "../escs/_index.md" >}}) and [T-Motor]({{< relref "../aerial-drones/t-motor.md" >}})).
